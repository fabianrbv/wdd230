The historic center of Bogotá

Located in the La Candelaria neighborhood , it preserves the memory of the small town that was Bogotá in its beginnings. Its narrow streets and colonial mansions are the nerve center of Bogotá culture . In addition to bringing together most of the tourist places of historical interest , this area is mixed with the modernity of commerce, universities and residential projects .

Colon Theater

In La Candelaria, inaugurated on October 27, 1892. Its architecture and decoration is inspired by the Garnier Theater in Paris . This venue is used for chamber concerts, conferences, plays and dance performances, among others.

Gold Museum

In the center there is also the Gold Museum , which has among its acquisitions gold, tumbaga, ceramics, stone and wood objects that were once the result of the most advanced and complex technology of the native groups of the American continent.

Monserrate Hill

To the east of the downtown area, the symbol par excellence of Bogotá, is located one of the most emblematic tourist places of the capital: the Monserrate hill. It is located 3,152 meters above sea level and houses the Sanctuary of the Fallen Lord at its summit , a place of religious pilgrimage. It has two luxurious restaurants, Casa San Isidro and Santa Clara , which offer a variety of national and international dishes.

La Macarena neighborhood

Towards the north, the National Museum is located . It was founded by law in 1823, which makes it the oldest museum in Colombia . Although it has had several locations, since 1948 it has been located in the old Cundinamarca Central Penitentiary, which was the most important prison in the country for almost 72 years . In addition to the interesting history of its facilities, the museum houses a wide collection of artistic pieces that tell much of the history of Colombia.

Andrés Carne de Res

In terms of gastronomy and nightlife , without a doubt Andrés Carne de Res is a must-see destination. Its main headquarters, located in Chía , a municipality 20 minutes from the capital, has been one of the favorites for more than two decades for foreigners and nationals looking to live a typical Colombian experience. This establishment was included in the book 333 places in Colombia to see before you die . Currently, the restaurant-bar has a branch in Bogotá: Andrés DC .

Salt Cathedral of Zipaquira

Regarding the options near the city, a visit to the Zipaquirá Salt Cathedral —considered the First Wonder of Colombia—is essential. This monument is built 180 meters underground in a large salt mine. Deep inside the majestic cathedral there is a gigantic 80-meter water mirror that offers a unique optical effect.
